Welcome to our design portfolio, where creativity and science collide. This area highlights our work in advanced optics, namely in the use of a modified Sagnac interferometer to generate re-configurable pairs of orthogonal cylindrical vector beams.

By enabling real-time switching between polarization vortex pairs, an innovation that improves efficiency in a variety of high precision applications, our study seeks to push the limits of optical communication, laser micromachining, and particle trapping.

Spherical Phase Plate (SPP) in Our Experiment

The Spherical Phase Plate (SPP) in our experiment is crucial for generating cylindrical vector beams (CVBs) by introducing an azimuthal phase delay, converting a Gaussian beam into a Laguerre-Gaussian (LG) mode with specific topological charge (l). Integrated into the Sagnac interferometer, the SPP enables the simultaneous production of orthogonal polarization vortices (radial and azimuthal beams), enhancing system efficiency and allowing real-time switching between vortex pairs. This capability is essential for applications like optical communication, laser micromachining, and particle trapping, making our method a novel and efficient approach for precise polarization control.

Role of BS1 and BS2 in Our Experiment

In our experiment, BS1 and BS2 are Beam Splitters that play a crucial role in the Sagnac interferometer setup.

  • BS1 (Beam Splitter 1): This component splits the incoming laser beam into two separate paths: one traveling clockwise (CW) and the other traveling counterclockwise (CCW). It ensures that both paths interact with the optical elements (SPP, QWP) before recombining.
  • BS2 (Beam Splitter 2): This beam splitter is responsible for recombining the CW and CCW beams after they have undergone phase modifications. It helps in forming the cylindrical vector beams (CVBs) by ensuring interference patterns align correctly and directing the output beams toward the final observation screen or detection system.

BS1 and BS2 enable the interference-based generation of polarization vortices, allowing the system to simultaneously generate two orthogonal cylindrical vector beams in different arms of the interferometer. Their precise function is essential for achieving real-time switching between polarization vortex pairs.

Half-Wave Plate (HWP) in Our Experiment

In our experiment, the Half-Wave Plate (HWP) is crucial for polarization control within the modified Sagnac interferometer, enabling the generation of orthogonal cylindrical vector beams such as radially and azimuthally polarized light. By rotating the input polarization state by twice its fast axis angle, the HWP ensures proper beam interference, alignment, and interaction with optical elements like polarizing beam splitters. This precise polarization tuning is essential for mode division multiplexing, optical trapping, and sensing applications. Since our setup operates in free space, a mounted, zero-order HWP that matches our laser’s wavelength is ideal for maintaining stability and minimizing wavelength and temperature dependencies.
