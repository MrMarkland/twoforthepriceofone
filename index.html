About the Project

This project explores a novel approach for generating and controlling polarization vortices using a modified Sagnac interferometer. Our research aims to enhance optical communication, mode division multiplexing, and laser micromachining.

Abstract

We propose and experimentally demonstrate a novel scheme generating pairs of orthogonal polarization vortices. A pair of radially and azimuthally polarized beams is produced in two Sagnac interferometer arms. The technique allows for reconfiguring the output polarization by rotating an intracavity wave plate or adjusting the input polarization.

Proposed Method

Our design uses a QWP and spiral phase plate (SPP) in a Sagnac interferometer. Adjusting the QWP angle or inserting an HWP at 45Â° enables switching between orthogonal polarization states.

Experimental Results

We used a 633 nm HeNe laser and a Viavi vortex phase plate for experimental validation. The results confirmed the theoretical predictions, successfully generating vortex beams of various topological charges.
