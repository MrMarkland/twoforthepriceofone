Welcome to our design portfolio, where creativity and science collide. This area highlights our work in advanced optics, namely in the use of a modified Sagnac interferometer to generate re-configurable pairs of orthogonal cylindrical vector beams.

By enabling real-time switching between polarization vortex pairs, an innovation that improves efficiency in a variety of high precision applications, our study seeks to push the limits of optical communication, laser micromachining, and particle trapping.

First Schematic: Basic Sagnac Interferometer Setup
This schematic represents the initial configuration of the Sagnac interferometer used to generate cylindrical vector beams (CVBs). The laser passes through a half-wave plate (HWP) and is split into clockwise (CW) and counterclockwise (CCW) paths by a beam splitter (BS1). The Spiral Phase Plate (SPP) and quarter-wave plate (QWP) help to generate polarization vortices, which are analyzed on the screen.
Second Schematic: Generation of First-Order Vortex Beams
This image demonstrates the formation of radially and azimuthally polarized beams, with distinct polarization vortices observed on the screen. The experimental setup remains the same, but the output patterns now show the expected polarization distribution for first-order vortices (l=1). The red arrows in the inset images on the screen represent the polarization direction of the generated beams.
Third Schematic: Higher-Order Vortex Beam Generation
The schematic shows how the system can be reconfigured to generate higher-order polarization vortices by adjusting the QWP and SPP. The screen displays more complex vortex structures, demonstrating the system’s ability to switch between different vector beam pairs. This highlights the Sagnac interferometer’s capability to produce tunable cylindrical vector beams for advanced optical applications.

Spherical Phase Plate (SPP) in Our Experiment

The Spherical Phase Plate (SPP) in our experiment is crucial for generating cylindrical vector beams (CVBs) by introducing an azimuthal phase delay, converting a Gaussian beam into a Laguerre-Gaussian (LG) mode with specific topological charge \( l \). Integrated into the Sagnac interferometer, the SPP enables the simultaneous production of orthogonal polarization vortices (radial and azimuthal beams), enhancing system efficiency and allowing real-time switching between vortex pairs. This capability is essential for applications like optical communication, laser micromachining, and particle trapping, making our method a novel and efficient approach for precise polarization control.
